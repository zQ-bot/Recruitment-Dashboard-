🕶️
Blind Evaluation
Name, photo, age, gender-coded terms, school name and address are masked before the AI model scores the resume. Reviewers can toggle identity back on only after scoring is complete.
📐
Evidence-Grounded Scoring
Every score is generated only from retrieved resume text, the job description, and the hiring rubric — never from the model's general assumptions about a candidate's background.
📊
Disparate-Impact Monitoring
Score distributions are compared across demographic groups weekly (post-hoc, not used in scoring). Gaps beyond policy threshold auto-open a review flag.

Open Bias Reviews

What triggers a Bias Indicator

  • Score volatility flag — AI Hiring Score shifts by more than 8 points between the blind and identity-visible pass for the same resume.
  • Proxy-variable flag — resume text contains signals that correlate with a protected class (e.g. graduation year implying age, name-based origin inference) that the model may have leaned on.
  • Cohort gap flag — a role's shortlist rate for one demographic cohort falls more than 2 standard deviations below the requisition average.
  • All flags route to a human recruiter for manual adjudication — the AI never auto-rejects on a bias flag.
